"There has never been a better time to have a problem."

The billboard is right — but the shift is bigger than "faster answers." It's a change in what a problem is.

For most of my career, a problem was a wall — something to research, debate, and eventually route around. Now a problem is a brief. You describe it. You constrain it. An hour later, something exists that didn't.

That reframe is what people mean when AI "changes how you work." It's not that you move faster. It's that you stop triaging which problems are worth solving — because suddenly, most of them are.

If you've been sitting on a problem because it felt too small to justify the effort, this is your sign to ship it.
